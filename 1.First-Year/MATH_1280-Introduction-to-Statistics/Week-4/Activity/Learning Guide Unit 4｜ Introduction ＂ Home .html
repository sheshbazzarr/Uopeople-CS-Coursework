Introduction

This unit introduces the concept of discrete random variables. The distributions of these random variables emerge as mathematical models of real-life settings.

For a discrete random variable, the sample space is composed of integers. Random variables in which the sample space is a collection of discrete values are called discrete random variables. This unit will cover the Binomial random variable, which is a type of discrete random variable.

For all the types of random variables that are considered, a formula for the expectation and a formula for the variance are given, each associated with its specific type of random variable.
